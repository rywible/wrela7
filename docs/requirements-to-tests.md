## Evidence policy

- Development evidence is fast and focused: strong types, representation
  validators, targeted properties, and the nearest consumer gate.
- Integration evidence uses the real native or image consumer once per landed
  capability and does not duplicate complete release-threat checks in every
  pure transformation crate.
- Release evidence is the expensive clean, path-distinct, adversarial,
  offline reproduction and publication route.
- Authority boundaries receive maximum validation; representation boundaries
  receive strong invariants. An already authenticated internal fact is not
  repeatedly remeasured unless a later authority boundary can invalidate it.
- Hand-built values prove a consumer or sealer only; they do not prove the
  producer.
- A producer unit test is incomplete until its output passes its real immediate
  consumer.
- Test doubles are valid only at injected host/process/native boundaries and
  require complementary real integration tests.
- A successful command that skips unavailable work is a failure, not evidence.
- Verification entries name the command, environment class, result, and the
  exact requirement rows advanced.
